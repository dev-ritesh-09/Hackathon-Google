Frequently Asked Questions

What is cybersecurity and why is it important?

Cybersecurity is the practice of protecting digital systems, networks, and sensitive data from unauthorized access, theft, or damage. As technology becomes more embedded in our daily lives, cybersecurity plays a vital role in keeping both individuals and organizations safe.

Without proper cybersecurity, personal data, financial records, and even critical infrastructure can be compromised. A single breach can lead to identity theft, business loss, or national level threats making cybersecurity not just important, but essential.

What are the most common types of cyber threats?

The most frequent cyber threats include phishing, malware, ransomware, DDoS (Distributed Denial of Service) attacks, and social engineering. These threats aim to steal data, disrupt systems, or demand ransom from victims.

Cybercriminals are constantly evolving their tactics, making it crucial to stay updated and cautious. Even clicking a single suspicious link can expose an entire system to risk.

How can I protect myself online?

Start by using strong, unique passwords and enabling two factor authentication on all your important accounts. Avoid clicking unknown links or downloading attachments from untrusted sources.

Keep your software and antivirus programs up to date to patch vulnerabilities. Practicing safe browsing habits and being cautious with personal data online goes a long way in protecting yourself.

What is phishing and how can I spot it?

Phishing is a tactic where attackers impersonate trusted sources like banks, companies, or friends—to trick you into revealing sensitive information, such as passwords or credit card details

You can often spot phishing by looking for poor grammar, suspicious email addresses, fake URLs, and urgent or threatening language. Always verify before clicking or sharing information.

What should I do if I think I've been hacked?

If you suspect a breach, act quickly. Disconnect from the internet, change your passwords immediately, and run a full antivirus or anti-malware scan.

Notify your bank, IT department, or relevant service providers if sensitive information may have been exposed. The faster you respond, the better your chances of minimizing the damage.
